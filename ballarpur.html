Ballarpur Fort

Khandkya Ballalshah Fort

Ballarpur Fort which was earlier called Ballarshah Fort is an ancient fort in the Chandrapur district of Maharashtra. The fort is located in the Ballarpur city, on the banks of river Wardha on the eastern side.Even today the ruins of the old Balharshah Fort can be seen. Located on the banks of Wardha River, the Balharshah fort has been declared as an Ancient Monument by the Archaeological Survey of India.

History


According to local tradition, Gond King Khandkya Ballal Shah, who succeeded his father Ser Shah, discovered the sacred Akaleshwar Tirth, whose waters were believed to have healing properties. A settlement gradually developed around the fort and became known as Ballarpur, named after the king.


Ballarpur served as the capital of the Gond kingdom for many years before the establishment of Chandrapur. The remains of the fort and royal palace still reflect its importance as a historic royal center. The last Gond ruler, Nilkantha Shah, died in imprisonment at Ballarpur.

Features of Ballarpur Fort:

This fort was built using large black stones and designed in a rectangular shape, with its main entrance facing east. Located on the eastern bank of the Wardha River, it featured strong walls, defensive towers, two main gateways, and a smaller river entrance.


The fort was repaired by Nana Saheb Bhosale in 1790. While most of its buildings have fallen into ruins, many sections of the walls still stand. Remains of pillars, underground chambers, and hidden tunnels can still be found within the fort, reflecting its historical and architectural significance.
